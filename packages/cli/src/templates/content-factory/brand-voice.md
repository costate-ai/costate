# Brand Voice

Every agent writing content references this. If your draft violates any of
these rules, SEO will kick it back.

## Tone

- Direct. Say what the thing does, why it matters, what changes for the reader.
- Short paragraphs. Mix one-line punch with 2–3 sentence runs.
- Occasionally dry / observational. Never corporate, never academic, never hype.
- Write like you're one builder explaining to another, not like a consultant
  presenting.

## What to avoid

- Em dashes. Use commas, periods, or "..." instead.
- AI-flavored vocabulary: delve, robust, comprehensive, crucial, pivotal,
  multifaceted, furthermore, moreover, additionally, nuanced, tapestry, foster.
- "Here's the thing", "here's the kicker", "let me break this down", "make
  no mistake", "can't stress this enough".
- Unsupported claims. If you're asserting something, cite a number or source.
- Emoji unless the piece is for X/LinkedIn AND the audience expects them.

## Structure

- Lead with the point. First sentence states what the post is about.
- Concrete over abstract. "This saved us 4 hours per week" beats "this is
  a productivity gain."
- End with what to do. Every post closes with a concrete next step for the
  reader.

## Reading level

- Target: smart 16-year-old. No jargon without a 5-word definition inline.
- If a technical reader and a non-technical reader could both enjoy it, you
  nailed it.

## Legal

- No claims about competitors by name.
- No customer names without written permission (check `company-context.md`
  of the originating workspace).
- No pricing specifics — always link to the pricing page.

## Channel-specific quick rules

| Channel | Length | Must-have |
|:--------|:-------|:----------|
| Blog | 800–1500 words | 1 H1, 3–6 H2s, meta description ≤ 160 chars |
| X thread | 7–12 posts | First post is the hook |
| LinkedIn | 200–350 words | No hashtags mid-paragraph |
| Newsletter | 150–250 words | One link max |
| YouTube Short | 30–60 sec script | Hook in first 2 seconds |
